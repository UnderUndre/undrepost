## Summary

The identity contract is the strong part: `sub`-authoritative (not email), tenant-claim validation → 403 + audit, idempotent JIT upsert (members.contract.md). SSE reliability defaults are now concrete, and money-state boundary has a schema-audit gate (T4.4). **But** the init's releasability is hard-blocked on an upstream (undrlla/003) that is itself CRITICAL, the data model can't actually represent two headline features (custom-emoji reactions, one-vote-per-user polls), and token validation + output sanitization are underspecified. Verdict: **CRITICAL**.

## Alternative approaches considered

- **Defer auctions to Wave 2** (F1) — un-blocks the content MVP from the CRITICAL undrlla upstream. The lead chose blocking; given undrlla/003's state, re-weigh: ship Wave 1 (free, standalone, real value) now, gate only the auction feature on undrlla.
- **Reaction = `(post, member, emojiRef)` with unique triple** (F2) instead of a fixed `type` enum — represents custom-emoji reactions and the uniqueness invariant in one model.
- **Explicit `PollVote` entity** (F3) instead of count-only `results` — the only way to enforce one-vote-per-user and to recount/audit.
- **Payload-native auth for Wave 1, swap to undrlla IdP at Wave 2** (F5) — makes the "temporary seam" concrete and truly decouples Wave-1 delivery from undrlla readiness.
